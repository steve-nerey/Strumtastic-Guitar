Lessons on notes

This chart shows you the different notes. At the bottom is a whole note, it's a four count. The two notes above it are half notes, they're each a two count. The four notes above it are quarter notes, they are each a one count. The eight notes above it are eighth notes. Two of these notes add up to one count. Eight of these notes take up the whole 4 count bar. The sixteen notes above it are sixteenth notes. Four of these notes add up to one count. sixteen of these notes take up the whole 4 count bar.

Lessons on reading chords and rhythm

The letters on top of the music are the chords you play on your guitar. The symbols on the bar are the rhythm notation. This tells you how the strumming goes on your guitar. The number on the left 4/4 is the timing of the song.

The notes on your strings

The chart above is as if you are looking down on it from your guitar. So the bottom E is the top string of your guitar. Looking at our chart below you will see that the 0 fret (open string) is the same as the 12th fret, only in a higher octave. Once you go past the 12th string the notes repeat the same cylcle, meaning fret 13 on the E string is the same note as fret 1 (F note) only in different octaves. Same rule applies to all strings.
